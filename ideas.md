<idea>
**Design Movement:** Alchemical Manuscript / Medieval Codex

**Core Principles:**
1. Every surface looks hand-crafted — parchment, vellum, worn leather
2. Ink bleeds and stains; nothing is perfectly crisp
3. Gold leaf accents emerge only at moments of significance
4. Depth through layering: paper on paper, wax seals, stitched bindings

**Color Philosophy:**
Warm ochre parchment (#E8D5A3) as the base — the color of aged paper held to candlelight. Deep sepia (#4A2C0A) for ink. Burnished gold (#C9A84C) for success glows. Muted clay red (#8B3A2A) for fire elements. Stone grey (#7A7060) for earth. The palette evokes a medieval alchemist's study, not a fantasy game.

**Layout Paradigm:**
Asymmetric desk layout — the fusion workspace is a worn wooden table surface. Elements are scattered like physical objects. The book opens from the right side. Battle arena feels like a parchment duel board laid flat. No centered grids; everything has slight rotational offset as if placed by hand.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Ink-bleed borders on cards — edges look hand-drawn with quill
2. Wax seal stamps for element categories
3. Torn parchment dividers between sections

**Interaction Philosophy:**
Drag feels like physically picking up a vial or stone. Drop triggers a soft "thud" or "clink." Fusion animations look like ink mixing in water — slow, organic diffusion.

**Animation:**
- Fusion: ink-drop diffusion, 600ms ease-out
- Page turn: realistic curl with slight crinkle
- Card reveal: slow unfold like unrolling a scroll
- Failure: elements bounce back with slight wobble, ink splatter particle

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Cinzel Decorative" (Roman serif, gold-accented) for titles
- Body: "IM Fell English" (period-appropriate serif with italic charm)
- UI labels: "Crimson Text" (readable old-style serif)
</idea>

<idea>
**Design Movement:** Brutalist Alchemical — Raw Material Meets Ancient Science

**Core Principles:**
1. Exposed structure — no decorative frills, only functional beauty
2. Heavy typography dominates; elements are typographic objects
3. Contrast is king: deep charcoal vs. raw amber
4. Texture from real materials: concrete, oxidized copper, raw linen

**Color Philosophy:**
Near-black charcoal (#1C1A17) as the primary canvas — the inside of a stone furnace. Oxidized copper (#B87333) as the primary accent. Pale linen (#F2ECD8) for text. Sulfur yellow (#D4A017) for fire. The palette feels industrial-ancient, like a forge that has been burning for centuries.

**Layout Paradigm:**
Grid-breaking asymmetry with heavy left-anchored columns. Elements stack like blocks of stone. The fusion workspace is a dark forge surface. Battle arena uses thick border lines as lane dividers — architectural, not decorative.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thick rule lines (4–6px) as structural dividers
2. Monospaced element names in uppercase
3. Oxidized metal texture overlays on interactive surfaces

**Interaction Philosophy:**
Interactions feel weighty and deliberate. Dragging an element has resistance. Successful fusion triggers a forge-spark burst. No smooth easing — snappy, mechanical transitions.

**Animation:**
- Fusion: spark burst + brief flash, 300ms
- Card reveal: hard flip, no easing
- Failure: sharp recoil, 150ms
- Lane reveal: slide-in from bottom like stone slabs

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Bebas Neue" (bold, condensed, industrial)
- Body: "Space Mono" (monospaced, technical)
- Labels: "Oswald" (condensed sans)

<idea>
**Design Movement:** Illuminated Manuscript + Soft Naturalism

**Core Principles:**
1. Warmth and softness — this world is alive, not cold
2. Organic shapes: no sharp corners, everything has a gentle curve
3. Layered depth through soft shadows and translucent overlays
4. Nature-forward: moss, bark, amber, clay — the elements feel real

**Color Philosophy:**
Warm parchment (#F5E6C8) as the base. Deep forest ink (#2D3B2A) for text. Soft gold (#D4A843) for accents and glows. Moss green (#6B7C5A) for nature elements. Clay red (#A0522D) for fire. Pale violet (#9B8EC4) for magic. The palette is a sunset seen through stained glass in an ancient library.

**Layout Paradigm:**
The main screen is a living desk — fusion workspace on the left, book on the right. Elements float in an organic cluster, not a rigid grid. The book has visible page curl. Battle arena is a central parchment board with soft vignette edges. Navigation feels like turning pages, not clicking tabs.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Soft inner glow on discovered elements (like candlelight from within)
2. Botanical ink illustrations as element icons
3. Vignette edges on every major surface — the world fades at its borders

**Interaction Philosophy:**
Everything breathes. Idle elements have a slow float animation. Hover triggers a warm glow. Drag feels like lifting a glass vial — smooth, slightly weighted. The book opens with a satisfying page-curl animation.

**Animation:**
- Fusion: warm light bloom expanding outward, 500ms ease-in-out
- Page turn: realistic 3D curl with paper texture, 400ms
- Card reveal: gentle unfold with soft glow edge, 350ms
- Element discovery: radial ink-bloom from center, 600ms
- Failure: gentle bounce-back with soft shake, 400ms

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Cinzel" (classical Roman caps, dignified)
- Body: "Lora" (warm humanist serif, highly readable)
- UI: "Crimson Pro" (elegant old-style for labels and stats)
